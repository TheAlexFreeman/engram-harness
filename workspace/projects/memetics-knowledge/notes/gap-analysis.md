## What is MISSING

1. **internet-meme-as-meta-format.md** — the popular sense of 'meme' as a template-based unit of internet humor. This is the most glaring gap. No file covers:
   - Divergence from Dawkins's original concept
   - The template/slot-filling structure (image macro, snowclone, exploitable)
   - Shifman's academic definition (content/form/stance + group of related items)
   - Participatory remix logic
   - Cognitive linguistics angle (Dancygier/Vandelanotte: construction grammar + blending)
   - Virality mechanisms specific to digital platforms

2. **meme-format-typology.md** — a taxonomy of internet meme formats:
   - Image macro (Impact font top/bottom captions)
   - Snowclone (verbal template with fill-in slots)
   - Exploitable (image with blank/modifiable region)
   - Reaction image/gif
   - Copypasta
   - Demotivational poster
   - Dank/absurdist memes (post-irony)
   - Video memes / audio memes

3. **Possible addition**: A bridge file on the Dawkins→internet-meme lexical split — how the word was appropriated, what Dawkins said about it, what it implies for the two research traditions.

## Answers to project questions

1. Existing files: listed above. Gaps: internet meme meta-format, format typology.
2. Relation to Dawkins: deliberate remix vs. random variation; Dawkins called internet memes a "hijacking." Key divergence: internet memes are *intentionally* transformed, not passively mutated.
3. Structural logic: template + variable slots + intertextual awareness. The meme IS the template; instances are uses of the template. Meta-format = format for generating formats.
4. Most authoritative treatments: Shifman 2014 (MIT Press) is the scholarly anchor; Dancygier & Vandelanotte 2017/2022 for linguistic analysis; also Knobel & Lankshear 2007.
5. Cognitive linguistics connections: blending (the image + caption form a blend), construction grammar (snowclones as constructions), frame semantics (the base image activates a frame; the caption violates or exploits it).
