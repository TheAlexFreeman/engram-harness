## Resolved questions (5)

1. ~What connections should be forged to cognitive linguistics (blending, construction grammar) and to existing Engram files?~
   → Cognitive linguistics connections: (1) Conceptual Blending (Fauconnier/Turner) — image + caption create a blend, the emergent meaning isn't in either alone; (2) Construction Grammar (Goldberg, Fillmore) — snowclones are constructions with fixed and variable slots, exactly parallel to grammatical constructions; (3) Frame Semantics (Fillmore/Sweetser) — the base image activates a rich frame; the caption subverts, exploits, or extends that frame; (4) Viewpoint/Stance — Dancygier & Vandelanotte show memes encode stances (irony, identification, distancing) via multimodal cues. Cross-link to cognitive-linguistics-metaphor-blending.md. (2026-04-25)
2. ~Which academic treatments of internet memes are most authoritative (Shifman, Dancygier/Vandelanotte, etc.)?~
   → Shifman (2014, MIT Press) is the primary scholarly anchor — defines internet memes as groups of digital items sharing content/form/stance, created with awareness of each other. Dancygier & Vandelanotte (2017 Cognitive Linguistics article; 2022 Cambridge book The Language of Memes) apply construction grammar and conceptual blending. Knobel & Lankshear (2007) introduced participatory culture framing. Milner (2016) on meme logic and politics. Varis & Blommaert (2015) on meme as sociolinguistic resource. (2026-04-25)
3. ~What is the structural logic of the internet meme as a meta-format (template, slot-filling, participatory remix)?~
   → The internet meme is a meta-format: a template with fixed and variable slots. The template (base image, verbal frame, structural pattern) is the meme; individual instances are uses of the meme. Participation = filling the slots. The format encodes a frame (via the base image or verbal pattern) and a stance (irony, absurdism, complaint, celebration); the instance inserts new content into that frame+stance, creating humor through frame clash, subversion, or recognition. Shifman's three dimensions: content (what is depicted), form (how it looks/sounds), stance (attitude/relationship to audience). (2026-04-25)
4. ~How does the popular 'internet meme' concept relate to (and diverge from) Dawkins's original memetics?~
   → Internet meme deliberately diverges: Dawkins himself said it is a "hijacking" of his concept. In biology-style memetics, memes mutate by random variation and spread through quasi-Darwinian selection. Internet memes are *intentionally* transformed by human creativity — the template is consciously remixed. The scholarly concept is about units of cultural transmission; the internet concept is about a participatory format for collective humor. They share surface vocabulary and some deep structure (variation, differential spread) but differ in mechanism and intentionality. (2026-04-25)
