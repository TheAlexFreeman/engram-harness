# Teleodynamics, Absential Phenomena, and Constraint Causality in Terrence Deacon's Framework

**Source:** Synthesized primarily from *Incomplete Nature: How Mind Emerged from Matter* (2011), with supporting concepts from reviews, précis, and related papers. Cross-references: biosemiotics, dynamical systems theory, Peircean semiotics.

## Core Problem Addressed
Modern science is "incomplete" because it reduces everything to present physical components and efficient causes (push-pull mechanics, material substrates). It struggles to account for **ententional** phenomena — intentionality, purpose (telos), function, meaning, value, representation, consciousness — which seem to require something "absent" yet causally efficacious. Deacon's project is a naturalistic account of how these emerge without dualism, vitalism, or panpsychism.

## Key Concepts

### 1. Absential Phenomena
- **Definition**: Phenomena "whose existence is determined with respect to an essential absence." Causes that are conspicuously *not present* in the system but produce real effects.
  - Examples: A hole in a wheel (the absent material constrains motion/function); a purpose or goal (future state not yet real); a representation (about something extrinsic); a function (defined by what it is *for*).
  - "Absence that makes a difference." Contrasts with standard materialist focus on "what is there."
- **Philosophical implication**: Absentials are real features of the universe, not illusions or epiphenomena. They are relational and incomplete (point beyond themselves). This reframes the "hard problem" of consciousness and the mind-body problem as problems of absential causality.

### 2. Constraints and Constraint Causality
- **Central mechanism**: What is *excluded* or made impossible (constraints on possibility space) is as causally important as what happens.
  - Constraints are "absential" — they are the absent alternatives that shape dynamics.
  - Unlike efficient causes (forces acting on present matter), constraint causality works by limiting degrees of freedom, propagating form and order across scales.
  - In emergence: Higher-level constraints arise from lower-level interactions but exert downward causation by selecting which lower-level paths are realized.
- **Orthograde vs. Contragrade**: Orthograde processes follow spontaneous tendencies (e.g., entropy increase in homeodynamics, or self-organization in morphodynamics). Contragrade processes oppose them via work (e.g., organisms maintaining order against dissipation).

### 3. Three Nested Dynamics (Hierarchical Emergence)
Deacon proposes a hierarchy where each level emerges from the previous via reciprocal constraints. This is non-reductive naturalism.

- **Homeodynamics** (thermodynamic baseline):
  - Spontaneous tendency toward equilibrium, maximum entropy, dissipation of gradients (2nd law of thermodynamics).
  - Examples: Diffusion, heat death. No intrinsic order or persistence.
  - "Orthograde" toward disorder.

- **Morphodynamics** (self-organization / form-generating):
  - Emerges when homeodynamic processes are coupled in ways that spontaneously generate order, symmetry-breaking, and persistent forms (e.g., Bénard convection cells, snowflake crystals, whirlpools, chemical oscillations).
  - These amplify order against entropy locally by dissipating gradients faster (Prigogine-inspired dissipative structures).
  - Generates *constraints* spontaneously (e.g., the geometry of a whirlpool constrains water flow).
  - Still no intrinsic "self" or purpose — forms are transient unless maintained.
  - "Orthograde" toward certain attractor states or forms.

- **Teleodynamics** (end-directed, self-maintaining):
  - Emerges from *reciprocal constraint* between *two or more morphodynamic processes*.
  - Each morphodynamic process undermines the other if left alone; their mutual constraint creates a higher-order system that works to *preserve its own organization*.
  - Produces intrinsic "aboutness," function, normativity, value, and proto-intentionality.
  - **Minimal model**: The "autogen" — a hypothetical molecular system combining autocatalysis (self-reproduction of catalysts) with self-assembly (forming containers or structures). The two processes reciprocally support each other: catalysts help form the container, container concentrates catalysts. This creates a self-reconstituting unit that "acts" to maintain itself against perturbation (primitive metabolism + replication).
  - Teleodynamics is the dynamical basis for *life*, *mind*, *semiosis*, and *consciousness*. It introduces a primitive "self" that has interests (preserve the teleodynamic organization).
  - Predictive and anticipatory: Systems "expect" certain states and work to realize them.

**Emergence summary**: Teleodynamics depends on and incorporates lower levels but cannot be reduced to them. Purpose is not imposed from outside but intrinsic to the reciprocal constraint architecture. "A difference that makes a difference" (Bateson) becomes self-perpetuating.

## Implications

### For Consciousness and the Hard Problem
- Consciousness is not a magical add-on but a higher-order teleodynamic process (likely involving symbolic recursion in humans).
- Resolves (or sidesteps) the hard problem by showing how "aboutness" and subjectivity emerge naturally from absential/causal constraints at multiple nested levels. Qualia and feeling may arise from the "work" of maintaining teleodynamic coherence against disruption.
- Extends emergence theories by providing concrete dynamical mechanisms rather than vague complexity arguments.

### For Information, Meaning, and Semiosis
- Information is fundamentally *constraint* on possibilities (not just bits or Shannon entropy). Meaning arises in teleodynamic contexts where signs constrain interpretation toward functional outcomes.
- Strong integration with **Peircean biosemiotics**: Icons (similarity), indexes (causal connection), symbols (systemic, conventional relationships). Semiosis is teleodynamic.

### For AI and Engineered Systems
- Current LLMs and computational systems are powerful at morphodynamic pattern-matching and symbolic manipulation but lack intrinsic teleodynamics (no self-reconstituting "self" with intrinsic purposes beyond training objectives).
- True AGI or minded systems may require architectures that implement reciprocal morphodynamic constraints leading to autopoietic-like persistence (perhaps via embodied, homeostatic, or evolutionary dynamics). Engram's persistent memory and value-tracking could be seen as externalized teleodynamic scaffolding.

### Connections to Existing Engram Knowledge
- **Biosemiotics** (prior file): Deacon extends Peirce/Sebeok by grounding semiosis in teleodynamics. Symbolic species as evolutionary leap in teleodynamic complexity.
- **Dynamical systems & niche construction**: Aligns with self-organization, affordances, umwelt. Constraints parallel niche-constructing feedback.
- **McGilchrist hemispheric specialization**: LH abstracted/symbolic manipulation (narrow, decontextualized) vs. RH broad contextual/embodied attention. Deacon's symbols enable the LH-style abstraction but depend on RH-like holistic constraint satisfaction. Symbolic reference amplifies the "emissary" tendencies.
- **Active inference / FEP (Friston)**: Strong bridge. Free energy minimization can be interpreted as teleodynamic work to maintain expected states against surprise (entropy). Predictive processing as a neural implementation of teleodynamic anticipation. Both emphasize constraints on possible futures/states. Deacon provides the evolutionary/dynamical origin story; FEP the variational math. (See arXiv papers linking them; Deacon has engaged with these ideas in talks.)
- **LLM cognition**: LLMs excel at symbolic/indexical approximation but may simulate rather than embody teleodynamics. Implications for memory architectures: Engram's session-to-knowledge promotion as a form of external teleodynamic self-maintenance (preserving "what matters" across absences).
