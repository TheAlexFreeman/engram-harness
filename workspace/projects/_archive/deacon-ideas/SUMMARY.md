## Resolved questions (5)

1. ~How do absential phenomena and constraints provide a non-reductionist account of purpose and information?~
   → Absential phenomena are causes defined by what is absent yet efficacious (e.g., a goal, a function, a representation). Constraints are the excluded possibilities that shape dynamics and propagate form/normativity (downward causation). Together they provide a non-reductionist yet fully naturalistic account: purpose and information are intrinsic to teleodynamic architecture (reciprocal morphodynamic constraints creating self-reconstitution), not imposed externally or illusory. Information redefined as constraint on possibilities (integrating Shannon-Boltzmann-Darwin). (2026-04-26)
2. ~What are the precise distinctions between iconic, indexical, and symbolic reference, and their evolutionary implications?~
   → Icons (resemblance/similarity), indexes (causal/spatiotemporal contiguity), symbols (systemic, relational, conventional — reference via position in a web of other signs). Symbols enable reference to the absent, hypothetical, and abstract; require learning a system of contrasts. Evolutionary implication: Crossing the symbolic threshold in hominins drove co-evolution of brain (prefrontal expansion) and language, creating the symbolic niche, cumulative culture, and human uniqueness. Lower levels bootstrap symbols. LLMs excel at symbolic pattern stats but lack full embodied/indexical grounding. (2026-04-26)
