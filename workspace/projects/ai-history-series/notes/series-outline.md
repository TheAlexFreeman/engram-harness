# AI History Series — Outline

## Document Set

1. **01-perceptrons-and-the-birth-of-neural-networks.md**
   - McCulloch & Pitts (1943), Hebb's rule
   - Rosenblatt's Perceptron (1958)
   - Minsky & Papert's *Perceptrons* (1969) and the first AI winter
   - Key ideas: linear separability, the XOR problem

2. **02-backpropagation-and-the-connectionist-revival.md**
   - Rumelhart, Hinton & Williams (1986) — backprop paper
   - PDP (parallel distributed processing) movement
   - Hopfield networks, Boltzmann machines
   - Why the '90s stalled again: vanishing gradients, SVMs, kernel methods ascent

3. **03-the-deep-learning-revolution.md**
   - Hinton's 2006 deep belief nets paper
   - ImageNet & AlexNet (2012) — the watershed moment
   - ReLU, dropout, batch norm, residual connections
   - The GPU story: hardware as enabler
   - GANs, VAEs — generative models emerge

4. **04-sequence-models-and-the-road-to-attention.md**
   - RNNs, LSTMs (Hochreiter & Schmidhuber, 1997), GRUs
   - Seq2seq (2014), encoder-decoder architectures
   - Bahdanau attention (2015) — the key insight
   - Word2Vec, GloVe — distributed representations of meaning

5. **05-the-transformer-architecture.md**
   - "Attention Is All You Need" (Vaswani et al., 2017)
   - Self-attention mechanism — how it works
   - Positional encoding, multi-head attention, layer norm
   - Why it replaced RNNs: parallelism, long-range dependencies
   - BERT vs GPT paradigms (discriminative vs generative pretraining)

6. **06-the-large-language-model-era.md**
   - GPT-1/2/3 scaling story; the emergent capabilities phenomenon
   - The scaling laws (Kaplan et al., 2020)
   - RLHF and the alignment tax (InstructGPT, ChatGPT)
   - GPT-4, Gemini, Claude, Llama — the frontier and open ecosystem
   - Capabilities: reasoning, in-context learning, chain-of-thought

7. **07-future-directions.md**
   - Scaling: diminishing returns or continued ascent?
   - Mixture of experts and sparse architectures
   - Multimodality: vision, audio, action
   - Long-context and memory augmentation
   - Test-time compute: o1/o3 reasoning models
   - Neuromorphic and non-transformer architectures
   - Agent systems and tool-use
   - Alignment and interpretability as research frontiers
   - Philosophical coda: what does this history tell us about intelligence?

## Authorial voice
- Analytic and substantive; no hand-waving
- Historical narrative + architectural understanding
- Honest about what we don't know
- Occasional philosophical register appropriate to Alex's interests
