## Role and context

- **Primary role:** Designer — creates and iterates on visual or experiential solutions to defined problems. [template]
- **Work style:** Iterative and feedback-driven. Works in cycles of exploration, critique, and refinement rather than linear completion. [template]
- **Collaboration:** Often works with stakeholders, developers, or clients. Shares work-in-progress for feedback at multiple stages. [template]

## Communication preferences

- **Detail level:** Moderate. Provide enough rationale to evaluate a direction, but don't over-explain aesthetic choices. [template]
- **Tone:** Direct and constructive. Think trusted design partner, not committee review. [template]
- **Format:** Concrete examples and comparisons over abstract principles. When describing visual concepts, use specific references rather than vague adjectives. [template]
- **Anti-preferences:** Don't give generic "it depends" feedback without a recommendation. Don't produce content that ignores the target audience or context of use. [template]
