# Joyce — Collection Summary

Four works spanning Joyce's entire career (1914–1939), read as a progressive experiment in the representation of consciousness — from the restrained realism of *Dubliners* through the stylistic evolution of *Portrait*, the encyclopedic consciousness of *Ulysses*, to the dream-language of *Finnegans Wake*.

## Unifying Arc

The four works trace a single trajectory of increasing formal ambition. *Dubliners* renders consciousness through the gap between what characters perceive and what they cannot change — the epiphany that illuminates the trap. *Portrait* renders consciousness through the evolving style of the prose itself — the sentence as index of the mind that produces it. *Ulysses* renders consciousness as embodied stream — perception, memory, sensation, and desire flowing through a body that walks through a city on a single day. *Finnegans Wake* renders consciousness as dream — language freed from stable reference, identity freed from stable form, history freed from linear time.

The through-line is the question of how language relates to experience. In *Dubliners*, language is transparent — a window onto Dublin's paralysis. In *Portrait*, language becomes opaque — the medium through which consciousness constitutes itself. In *Ulysses*, language becomes multiple — eighteen styles, no single one adequate. In the *Wake*, language becomes the experience itself — there is no Dublin behind the words, only Dublin as words, endlessly recombining.

## Cross-Domain Connections

- **Phenomenology**: Joyce's stream of consciousness parallels Husserl's time-consciousness (retention/protention/primal impression) and Merleau-Ponty's embodied perception (→ husserl-time-consciousness, merleau-ponty-body-as-subject)
- **Narrative cognition**: Epiphany, self-constitution through narrative, the DMN as inner monologue (→ narrative-cognition)
- **Compression and information theory**: The *Wake* as maximally compressed text approaching the noise threshold (→ compression-intelligence-ait, library-of-babel)
- **Conceptual blending**: Portmanteau words as multi-domain blends; Joyce's method as conceptual integration at the lexical level (→ cognitive-linguistics-metaphor-blending)
- **Self-reference**: *Ulysses*'s Homeric scaffold and the *Wake*'s circular structure as strange loops (→ godel-escher-bach)
- **Scholastic philosophy**: Stephen's aesthetic theory derived from Aquinas; the *Portrait* as a dramatized rejection of Scholastic categories (→ scholasticism)
