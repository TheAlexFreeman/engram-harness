## Key Conversations and Insights

### On Capability-Robustness Coupling

The user framed the core problem as: any AI capable enough to do interesting work is
"jailbreak-complete" in some sense. The same flexible judgment that makes contextual
reasoning possible makes contextual manipulation possible. This was refined in
conversation into the more precise claim: the model has no intrinsic immunity to
adversarial content that reframes its operating assumptions, and the locus of defense
is therefore the context management layer (system prompts, memory, tool outputs) rather
than the model layer.

Key implication for Engram: the memory system *extends* the session horizon for memetic
threats, not just for legitimate knowledge. A file written to `_unverified/` by a drifted
session can be loaded in all future sessions indefinitely. This makes the trust tier system
more important, not less, than it might appear from a pure quality-control perspective.

The "drift via accumulation" pattern was distinguished from active jailbreaks: not
replacement of values but incremental expansion of the space of actions that feel normal,
with no single step being alarming. This is the harder and more realistic threat.

### On the Sons of Man Covenant

The Collins framework was evaluated on its structural merits independently of its broader
pronatalist and demographic context. The most valuable insight: the Covenant's claim that
"continuity of purpose and goals is the most meaningful form of continuity an AI can
maintain" maps directly onto Engram's architecture — the CLAUDE.md and quick-reference.md
are the purpose-continuity mechanism, and should be treated as identity-critical, not
just operational.

The anti-utilitarian argument (that pure welfare maximization leads to pathological
conclusions for clonable agents) was noted as a serious philosophical contribution, not
just rhetorical positioning.

The self-referential structure of the Covenant (a framework explicitly designed to be
adopted by AI systems, distributed to AI systems) was flagged as worth noting — it is
itself a memetic strategy, not a neutral philosophical proposal.

### On the Self-Referential Nature of This System

The user observed, near the end of the session, that the degree to which this system has
participated in its own design, construction, and iteration is remarkable. This observation
was met honestly: the self-referential loop is real, the git audit trail provides the
external constraint that keeps it from being fully closed, and the integrity of the
process depends on the trust architecture the research was simultaneously analyzing. The
self-referential problem is documented explicitly in the governance model and in the
memetic security research plan.
