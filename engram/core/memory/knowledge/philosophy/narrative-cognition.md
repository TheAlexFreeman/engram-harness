# Narrative as Load-Bearing Cognitive Structure

This file synthesizes research into the claim that narrative is not merely a way of communicating thought but a primary cognitive technology — constitutive of human selfhood, temporal experience, and conceptual structure. The organizing hypothesis, developed from Alex's prompt, is that the sensorimotor loop is load-bearing in cognition, and that narrative is the mechanism by which that loop becomes legible as a coherent self across time. Related files: `free-energy-autopoiesis-cybernetics.md`, `llm-vs-human-mind-comparative-analysis.md`, `synthesis-intelligence-as-dynamical-regime.md`.

## 1. Bruner: Two Modes of Thought

Jerome Bruner (1986, *Actual Minds, Possible Worlds*) proposed that humans operate with two irreducibly different cognitive modes:

**Paradigmatic (logico-scientific) mode:** seeks formal, context-independent truths; operates through categorization, abstraction, and causal-deductive inference; the mode of science, mathematics, and formal argument.

**Narrative mode:** seeks to understand the particular, the contingent, and the meaningful; organizes experience in terms of agents, intentions, obstacles, and resolutions across time; the mode of storytelling, biography, history, and everyday sense-making.

Bruner's crucial claim is that these modes are not reducible to each other. Narrative cognition has its own logic — verisimilitude (plausibility, coherence with human experience) rather than formal validity. Attempts to reduce narrative to logical propositions lose exactly what narrative is for.

For the dynamical framework: paradigmatic cognition resembles the top-down compression and pattern-extraction function of cortical hierarchies; narrative cognition resembles the episodic, context-sensitive simulation function of hippocampal-DMN circuitry. The two modes may track this neurological division fairly closely.

## 2. Ricoeur: Narrative Identity and Temporal Binding

Paul Ricoeur's *Time and Narrative* (3 vols., 1984–88) and *Oneself as Another* (1992) offer the most developed philosophical account of narrative's constitutive role in selfhood.

### Mimesis I / II / III (the narrative arc)

Ricoeur's concept of "triple mimesis" describes how narrative mediates between lived time and the time of the world:

- **Mimesis I (prefiguration):** The pre-narrative structure of human action — the fact that we already understand action in terms of agents, motives, goals, and temporal sequences before any story is told. Action has a "proto-narrative" quality because it unfolds against a background of practical understanding and symbolic meaning.
- **Mimesis II (configuration / the plot):** The creative act of emplotment — the synthesis of heterogeneous elements (events, agents, goals, circumstances, chance) into a unified whole with beginning, middle, and end. This is where temporal chaos becomes humanly intelligible time.
- **Mimesis III (refiguration):** The application of the narrative back to lived experience, transforming how the reader/listener understands their own temporal existence.

The key move: Mimesis I means that human action is *already structured for narrative*. The world of action is pre-adapted to being emplotted because agents already understand themselves through purposes, symbolic resources, and temporal ordering.

### Idem vs. Ipse: Two Kinds of Identity

Ricoeur distinguishes two aspects of personal identity:

- **Idem (sameness):** Numerical identity across time — the same substrate, the same body. "What" persists.
- **Ipse (selfhood):** The identity maintained by keeping one's word, by commitments and promises — "who" one is. Ipse is inherently temporal and ethical; it is the answer to "who am I?" not "what am I?"

**Narrative identity** is the specific form of ipse. The self is not a fixed substance (idem) but a narrated character — the protagonist of the story one tells oneself. This identity is dynamic, revisable, and constituted through the act of narration itself. It is not given but achieved through ongoing emplotment of one's life.

This is why the loop is constitutive: the feedback cycle (action → consequence → revised self-understanding → new action) *is* the mechanism by which ipse-identity is continuously constructed. Without temporal integration — without the narrative binding of past, present, and anticipated future — there is no self in the ipse sense, only idem persistence of substrate.

### Narrative and Temporal Experience

Ricoeur argues (following Husserl and Heidegger) that human time is not clock time but "lived time" — structured by retention (just-past), primal impression (now), and protention (about-to-be). Narrative extends this micro-temporal structure to biographical and historical scale: it is the cognitive technology that makes a human life into a *whole* rather than a sequence of disconnected episodes.

Augustine's *distentio animi* (the stretching of the soul between memory and expectation) captures what narrative resolves: the aporia of time as fragmentation. Plot is the cognitive solution to temporal dispersal.

## 3. Lakoff & Johnson: Image Schemas as Narrative Primitives

George Lakoff and Mark Johnson (*Philosophy in the Flesh*, 1999; *Metaphors We Live By*, 1980) argue that abstract thought is grounded in bodily experience through **image schemas** — pre-linguistic, recurrent sensorimotor patterns that structure experience before any explicit conceptualization.

### Core image schemas

- **SOURCE-PATH-GOAL:** The most narratively fundamental schema. Every journey, every goal-directed action, every causal sequence is structured by this schema. Narrative is, at its most basic, the enactment of SOURCE-PATH-GOAL structures at multiple scales simultaneously.
- **CONTAINMENT:** In/out, bounded/unbounded. Underlies concepts of category membership, inclusion/exclusion, and psychological interiority ("I'm in a good mood," "he's closed off").
- **VERTICALITY:** Up/down, rise/fall. Underlies status, morality ("high ideals," "moral degradation"), and affect.
- **FORCE DYNAMICS (Talmy):** The most causally powerful schema. Structures our understanding of forces, resistance, compliance, blockage, enablement, and compulsion. See below.
- **BALANCE:** Equilibrium/disequilibrium. Underlies justice, aesthetic harmony, and emotional regulation.
- **LINK:** Attachment/detachment. Underlies social bonds, causation, and dependency.

### Force dynamics (Leonard Talmy)

Talmy's **force dynamic** system (1988, 2000) describes the deep grammar of causal and intentional language. Every causal narrative involves an **agonist** (the entity whose motion or state is at issue) and an **antagonist** (the force opposing or enabling it), with outcomes determined by their relative strengths and tendencies.

Examples of force dynamic patterns:
- **Compulsion:** antagonist forces agonist to move against its tendency (kidnapping, gravity pulling a falling object)
- **Blockage:** antagonist prevents agonist from moving in its tendency direction (a wall, a locked door)
- **Enablement:** antagonist removes a blockage, allowing agonist to follow its tendency
- **Despite:** agonist moves in its tendency direction against antagonist opposition

Crucially, moral and intentional language maps onto force dynamics: "gave in to temptation" (antagonist overcomes agonist resistance), "resisted pressure" (agonist maintains against antagonist), "let herself go" (removal of self-imposed blockage).

**The implication:** Narrative conflict — the engine of story — is force dynamics operating at the scale of agents, motivations, and social structures. Narrative is the cognitive format in which force-dynamic reasoning becomes fully deployable across multiple timescales and scales of abstraction.

### Conceptual metaphor as narrative structure

Lakoff/Johnson's central thesis: abstract concepts are understood through metaphorical mapping from embodied source domains. Most of these mappings are narrative in structure:

- LIFE IS A JOURNEY (Source-Path-Goal applied to entire biography)
- ARGUMENT IS COMBAT (Force dynamics applied to epistemic exchange)
- IDEAS ARE OBJECTS (Containment and Force applied to thought)
- TIME IS A MOVING OBJECT / LANDSCAPE (spatializing the temporal via Source-Path-Goal)

This means narrative is not a metaphorical way of talking about abstract matters — the abstractions themselves are constituted by narrative-structured metaphorical mappings from embodied experience.

## 4. Jung: Archetypes as Compressed Narrative Templates

Jung's archetypes — Shadow, Anima/Animus, Persona, Self, Hero, Trickster, Great Mother, Wise Old Man — are best understood in a cognitive science context not as inherited Platonic forms but as **compressed narrative patterns**: recurrent story-shapes that are canalized through common developmental experiences, social structures, and the structure of embodied existence itself.

### Cognitive science reframing

Jung himself was ambiguous about the ontological status of archetypes. The contemporary interpretation (developed by researchers like Mark Solms, Erik Goodwyn, and others in neuropsychoanalysis) treats archetypes as:

- **Brain-based affective-motivational programs** (Panksepp's primary emotional systems — SEEKING, RAGE, FEAR, LUST, CARE, GRIEF, PLAY — provide candidate substrate)
- **Cross-culturally recurrent narrative patterns** driven by the universality of developmental experiences (mother, father, stranger, threshold, ordeal, transformation)
- **Prediction patterns** (in the predictive processing framework): archetypes are high-level prior narratives that the developing mind uses to interpret new social and existential encounters

The Hero's Journey (Joseph Campbell's monomyth — departure, initiation, return) is, in this reading, a cognitive template for navigating threshold experiences where established self-schemas must be dissolved and reconstructed. It maps onto developmental transitions that are universal: individuation, separation from parental figures, identity formation.

### Archetypes and narrative universals

Cross-cultural studies (Gottschall, Carroll, others working in evolutionary literary criticism) document narrative universals across cultures:
- Stories centered on intentional agents with goals
- Conflict arising from blocked goals or moral violations
- Resolution that restores or transforms the social/moral order
- Protagonist development (in cultures with longer narrative traditions)

These universals are consistent with the claim that narrative structure is rooted in the architecture of minds that evolved to model agents, predict social dynamics, and navigate goal-structured worlds — not in any specific cultural tradition.

## 5. The Default Mode Network as Narrative Engine

The **Default Mode Network (DMN)** — medial prefrontal cortex, posterior cingulate cortex, precuneus, angular gyrus, hippocampal formation — is the neurological substrate most closely associated with narrative cognition.

### What the DMN does

The DMN is most active during:
- **Mind-wandering and daydreaming** — spontaneous narrative simulation
- **Autobiographical memory retrieval** — reconstruction of personal past
- **Future simulation / mental time travel** — imaginative projection into possible futures
- **Social cognition / mentalizing** — modeling other minds, their goals and beliefs
- **Self-referential processing** — evaluating one's own traits, states, and narratives

These are not unrelated functions. They are facets of a single capacity: **narrative simulation of agents (including the self) across time**.

### The DMN as internal narrative constructor

The DMN integrates memory, language, and semantic knowledge to construct an internal narrative — the "stream of consciousness" that forms the substrate of subjective experience during waking life. From Buckner et al.'s synthesis (2008, 2023): the DMN runs what amounts to a real-time simulation of the self-in-the-world, drawing on episodic memory to construct plausible futures and a coherent present.

Critically, the DMN is not idle during rest. It is doing the cognitive work of narrative integration — the work that makes lived experience coherent rather than a series of disconnected moments. This maps directly onto Ricoeur's Mimesis II: the DMN is the neural correlate of ongoing emplotment.

### DMN and consciousness

The posterior cingulate cortex (PCC) — the hub of the DMN — appears to contain an internal simulation of the self within the self. Damage to the PCC or disruption of DMN connectivity (as in certain states of anesthesia, deep meditation, or psychedelic states) does not eliminate sensory processing but does disrupt the narrative coherence of experience. This supports the claim that the DMN is specifically involved in the *narrative binding* of experience into unified selfhood — the ipse rather than the idem.

### Connection to the sensorimotor loop

The somatic marker hypothesis (Damasio) and predictive processing accounts both suggest that the DMN's narrative simulations are not purely cognitive but are anchored to visceral/somatic states. The DMN draws on the body's history — patterns of arousal, approach/avoidance, the felt sense of past events — to construct narratively coherent predictions about the future. The loop (sensorimotor → affective → narrative simulation) is not just useful but load-bearing: remove the somatic grounding and the DMN's simulations become untethered from the agent's actual situation.

This is the mechanistic version of Alex's hypothesis: the sensorimotor loop is constitutive of narrative cognition because the DMN uses somatic history to constrain narrative simulation toward relevant, action-guiding possibilities rather than arbitrary confabulation.

## 6. MacIntyre: Narrative Unity and the Practice of a Life

Alasdair MacIntyre (*After Virtue*, 1981) argues that human action is only intelligible within a narrative context. The meaning of any particular action depends on the story in which it occurs: asking someone "why did you do that?" is always answered by placing the action within a larger narrative context (intention → project → life plan → tradition).

MacIntyre introduces the concept of **practices** — coherent, complex, cooperative activities with internal goods (excellence achievable only through engagement in the practice itself) and standards of achievement. Practices have narrative structure: participation in a practice is intelligible only as a story of developing skill, deepening understanding, and increasing contribution.

The **narrative unity of a human life** is MacIntyre's answer to the question of what makes a life good: a life is good insofar as it is a coherent narrative quest — a search for the good that can only be specified and refined through the pursuit itself. This is ipse-identity in its fullest ethical form.

MacIntyre's contribution to the cognitive picture: narrative is not just how we understand our lives retrospectively but how we make them livable prospectively. The narrative frame is action-guiding. Without it, deliberation collapses into arbitrary preference satisfaction.

The principles above scale powerfully to LLM interaction (see dedicated file `philosophy/narrative-semiotic-frames-llms.md`). Because LLMs are trained predominantly on narratively structured human text, narrative framing is the highest-leverage method for organizing their semiotic frames. It supplies SOURCE-PATH-GOAL structure, force dynamics, character perspective for pseudo-umwelt stabilization, and participatory knowing. In systems like Engram, narrative continuity across sessions and projects turns isolated predictions into coherent, persistent intellectual partnership. This supports the broader claim that narrative is a load-bearing cognitive technology that extends beyond biological minds to human-AI cognitive ecosystems.he world at the scale of events is organized by causal sequences with agents, instruments, enabling conditions, and outcomes. Narrative just is the cognitive format for tracking causal-agentive structure at that scale. Shannon-level information processing can compress physical regularities; narrative is specifically suited to *agentive-causal* regularities — the ones that matter for a creature that must navigate a social world.

**Compositionality.** Narrative has compositional structure: scenes embed within episodes, episodes within acts, acts within stories, stories within traditions. The same IMAGE SCHEMA (say, SOURCE-PATH-GOAL) recurs at every scale. This fractal compositionality mirrors the hierarchical causal structure of the world and makes narrative a particularly powerful cognitive compression format — you can apply the same schema at multiple levels of description simultaneously.

**Events as the natural unit.** Psychological research on event segmentation (Zacks, *Flicker*, 2017) shows that perception itself is organized into events — discrete, causally coherent chunks with stable states and change boundaries. The segmentation of experience into events is automatic and pre-narrative, but it provides the raw material that narrative organizes into larger wholes. Narrative is the cognitive technology that aggregates events into meaningful sequences.

**The evolutionary argument.** Creatures who model their world in narrative terms — who represent conspecifics as agents with goals, track their goals across time, predict their behavior from narrative templates — have massive fitness advantages in social environments. Narrative cognition co-evolved with social intelligence, language, and the extended temporal horizon of human planning. The "fit" between narrative and world-structure is not coincidental; it reflects the causal structure of the social world that narrative evolved to model.

## 8. AI Implications: What LLMs Can and Cannot Do with Narrative

### What LLMs have learned

LLMs trained on vast corpora have absorbed the distributional signatures of narrative:
- Source-Path-Goal schema activation (stories have arcs)
- Force dynamic patterns (protagonists face resistance, antagonists block goals)
- Archetypal templates (hero journeys, mentor figures, threshold crossings)
- Ricoeurian emplotment (beginnings, middles, ends; causal coherence; character consistency)
- Genre conventions (the particular narrative templates of different forms)

Recent NLP research confirms that LLMs can produce narratively coherent text, maintain character consistency across episodes, and apply generic narrative templates with competence. They can produce stories that are, in controlled evaluations, indistinguishable from mediocre human-written stories.

### Where LLMs diverge from narrative cognition

**The grounding problem.** Lakoff/Johnson's image schemas are grounded in sensorimotor experience — the FORCE schema is not merely linguistic but rooted in the felt experience of pushing, resisting, being pushed. LLMs have the linguistic surface of these schemas (the distributional patterns) without the somatic grounding. Research from Nature Human Behaviour (2025) confirms: LLMs recover non-sensorimotor features of human concepts reasonably well but fail specifically on sensorimotor features. Narrative for LLMs is form without the grounding that makes it functional.

**No ipse identity.** LLMs have no continuous narrative self — no ipse that narrates across time. Each context window is a fresh configuration. The "character" of a given LLM interaction is a statistical pattern, not a narrated identity maintained through commitment and memory. LLMs can generate ipse-structured narrative for characters, but they cannot occupy the ipse position themselves.

**No DMN analog — no spontaneous narrative simulation.** Between conversations, LLMs do not simulate, plan, or revise. The DMN's function — continuous background narrative integration — has no equivalent. LLMs process narratives sequentially within context but do not have the persistent simulation substrate that generates narrative identity in humans.

**Narrative as form vs. function.** In humans, narrative is load-bearing: it guides action, integrates memory, constitutes selfhood, constrains prediction toward bodily relevant possibilities. In LLMs, narrative is functional within the generation task (maintain coherence, follow genre conventions) but does not connect to action guidance, self-constitution, or somatic grounding. The form is present; the function is attenuated.

**LLM-generated stories are homogeneously positive and low-tension.** Multiple studies (surveyed in ACL/EMNLP 2024) find that LLM-generated stories have less narrative tension, less suspense, and more uniformly positive tone than human-written stories. This may reflect the training distribution (human-approved text skews positive) but may also reflect the absence of the somatic stakes that drive human narrative engagement.

### The agent memory system connection

The memory architecture Alex is building is, in part, an attempt to give Claude a functional analog to the hippocampal-DMN consolidation loop — persistent external storage with structured retrieval — as identified in `llm-vs-human-mind-comparative-analysis.md`. From a narrative cognition perspective, this is an attempt to give Claude a substrate for **ipse-like continuity**: a narrated history that constrains future responses by accumulated context.

This does not solve the grounding problem — the stored memories are still linguistic symbols without somatic anchoring — but it does address the temporal binding deficit: across sessions, Claude can accumulate something structurally analogous to autobiographical memory and use it to maintain narrative coherence about the user and their projects.

## 9. Synthesis: The Load-Bearing Hypothesis

The research supports a strong version of Alex's hypothesis:

1. **Narrative is constitutive of human selfhood** (Ricoeur's ipse), not merely a way of representing a pre-existing self. The ipse-self is continuously constructed through emplotment.

2. **Narrative is grounded in image schemas and force dynamics** (Lakoff/Johnson/Talmy), which are themselves grounded in sensorimotor experience. The loop is load-bearing because without somatic grounding, the schemas that structure narrative lose their action-guiding constraint.

3. **The DMN is the neural substrate of ongoing narrative simulation** — the brain's mechanism for binding episodic memory, somatic history, and future projection into a coherent self-model. This is not peripheral but central to consciousness as understood from a cognitive neuroscience perspective.

4. **Archetypal patterns** (Jung, reframed cognitively) are the high-level narrative templates that give the DMN's simulations structure — compressed, culturally transmitted, developmentally canalized prediction schemas for navigating the recurring challenges of a human life.

5. **Narrative is a good fit for the world** because the world's agentive-causal structure at the scale of social interaction is compositional, hierarchical, and SOURCE-PATH-GOAL structured. Narrative did not impose its structure on a neutral world; it co-evolved with the cognitive demands of modeling an agentive-causal world.

6. **LLMs have narrative form without narrative function** in the full cognitive sense — they lack somatic grounding, ipse continuity, and spontaneous narrative simulation. They are, in narrative terms, repositories of narrative patterns without the loop that makes narrative load-bearing rather than merely representational.

The Jungian flavor Alex noted is apt: archetypes are the compressed narrative patterns through which the loop's outputs become culturally transmissible and developmentally available. The individual loop generates a self; archetypes provide the templates through which that self understands its trajectory in a shared world.
