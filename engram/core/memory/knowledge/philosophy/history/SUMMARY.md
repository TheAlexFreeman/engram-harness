# History of Philosophy — Broad Survey

This directory holds the output of `philosophy-history-survey (historical plan reference)`. Files are organized by period and tradition, each tracking four cross-cutting through-lines:

1. **Mind, knowledge, and world** — epistemology, philosophy of mind, perception, skepticism
2. **Language and meaning** — the relationship between thought, language, and reality
3. **Ethics, politics, and the self** — practical philosophy, selfhood, community
4. **Science, metaphysics, and religion** — the status of knowledge claims, the boundaries between science, philosophy, and theology

## Phase 1 — The Greek Foundation ✓ 4/4 complete

- **`ancient/pre-socratics.md`** — The first philosophers (c. 600–400 BCE): the shift from mythos to logos; Heraclitus (flux, logos) vs. Parmenides (being, the One); Democritus and atomism. The opposition of permanence and change that runs through the entire Western tradition.
- **`ancient/plato.md`** — The Theory of Forms; epistemology and the cave; Socratic ethics; politics and the Republic; later self-critique (the Parmenides); the Timaeus cosmology. The founding document of Western idealism.
- **`ancient/aristotle.md`** — Aristotle's systematic critique of Plato; hylomorphism and the four causes; logic (the Organon); *De Anima* and the soul as form of the body; eudaimonia and virtue ethics; the *Poetics* as the first narrative theory. The founding document of Western naturalism.
- **`ancient/hellenistic.md`** — Philosophy as therapy after Alexander: Epicureans (atoms, tranquility, the Garden); Stoics (logos, virtue, the dichotomy of control); Pyrrhonian skeptics (epoché, the five modes of Agrippa); Neo-Platonists (the One, emanation, contemplation). The bridge to medieval thought.

## Phase 2 — The Medieval Synthesis ✓ 3/3 complete

- **`medieval/augustine-neoplatonism.md`** — Augustine's Christianization of Neo-Platonism; time and eternity (*Confessions* Book XI); the will, original sin, and grace; the City of God; inner illumination. The template for medieval Christian philosophy.
- **`medieval/islamic-jewish-transmission.md`** — How Greek texts survived and were substantially developed in the Islamic world; Al-Farabi, Avicenna (the Floating Man), Averroes, Al-Ghazali's response, Maimonides. The intellectual transmission that shaped Western Scholasticism.
- **`medieval/scholasticism.md`** — The Scholastic synthesis of faith and Aristotelian reason; Anselm's ontological argument; Aquinas's five ways and natural law; Duns Scotus on haecceity; Ockham's nominalism and the seeds of dissolution.
