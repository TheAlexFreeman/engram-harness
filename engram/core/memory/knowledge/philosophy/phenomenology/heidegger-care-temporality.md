# Heidegger: Care, Temporality, and Authenticity

## Overview

The concepts of **Care** (Sorge), **temporality** (Zeitlichkeit), and **authenticity** (Eigentlichkeit) form the culminating argument of *Being and Time* Division I and the bridge to Division II. Care is revealed as the unified ontological structure of Dasein's being; temporality is disclosed as the meaning of Care; and authenticity is the existential modification in which Dasein own its temporal existence. Together, they constitute Heidegger's deepest account of what it means to be the kind of being we are.

## Care (Sorge) as the Being of Dasein

### The Structural Definition

Heidegger defines Care as: **"ahead-of-itself-being-already-in-(a-world-) as being-alongside (entities encountered within-the-world)"** (BT §41).

This unwieldy formula captures three structural moments:

| Moment | German | Meaning | Temporal dimension |
|--------|--------|---------|-------------------|
| **Existentiality** | Sich-vorweg-sein | Ahead-of-itself: projection into possibilities | Future |
| **Facticity** | Schon-sein-in | Already-in: thrownness into a situation | Past |
| **Fallenness** | Sein-bei | Being-alongside: absorption in the world | Present |

**Care is not an emotion** (not "caring about" something). It is the formal structure that makes all particular concerns, interests, worries, and involvements possible. Dasein *cares* in the sense that its being is at issue for it — it matters to itself.

### Two Forms of Concern

- **Besorgen** (concern): Dasein's practical dealings with equipment and things (ready-to-hand engagement). "Concern" in the sense of attending to tasks, projects, and worldly involvements.
- **Fürsorge** (solicitude): Dasein's being-with others. Not charity but the structural relationship to other Daseins — which can be authentic (helping others become free for their own possibilities) or inauthentic (dominating, managing, taking over for others).

### Care and Worldhood

Care is not added on top of being-in-the-world. It *is* being-in-the-world grasped in its structural unity. The referential totality of equipment (Zuhandenheit) is ultimately grounded in Care: things matter, have significance, and form referential chains *because* Dasein's being is at issue for it.

The "for-the-sake-of-which" (Worumwillen) — the ultimate point of the referential chain — is always Dasein's own being. The hammer is for nailing, the nailing is for building, the building is for shelter, the shelter is *for the sake of* Dasein's ability to be.

## Temporality (Zeitlichkeit) as the Meaning of Care

### The Question

If Care is the being of Dasein, what is the *meaning* of Care? What makes Care possible as a unified structure? Heidegger's answer: **temporality**.

### Ecstatic Temporality

Temporality is not "clock time" (Heidegger calls that **Innerzeitigkeit**, within-time-ness — a derived, leveled-down form of temporality). Authentic temporality has three **ecstases** (from Greek *ek-stasis* — "standing outside of"):

| Ecstasis | Care moment | Character |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Future** (Zukunft) | Existentiality (projection) | Coming-toward-itself: Dasein's running ahead toward possibilities |
| **Having-been** (Gewesenheit) | Facticity (thrownness) | Having-been: taking up the thrown past |
| **Present** (Gegenwart) | Fallenness (being-alongside) | Making-present: encountering entities in the current situation |

**Ecstatic** means: temporality is not a container in which things happen. It is the *self-extending* of Dasein into future, past, and present simultaneously. Dasein is not *in* time; Dasein *temporalizes* — it generates temporality through its own existence.

### Primacy of the Future

Heidegger asserts the **primacy of the future**: the future ecstasis is primary because Dasein is fundamentally ahead-of-itself (projection). We understand ourselves first in terms of what we *will be*, not what we *are* (present) or *were* (past).

This is the reverse of ordinary understanding, which privileges the past (causes) or the present (current state). For Heidegger, Dasein's being is constituted by its orientation toward possibilities.

### Authentic vs. Inauthentic Temporality

| Feature | Authentic temporality | Inauthentic temporality |
|---------|----------------------|------------------------|
| Future | Anticipation (Vorlaufen): running ahead to death as ownmost possibility | Awaiting (Gewärtigen): expecting future events within the world |
| Past | Repetition (Wiederholung): actively taking up thrown possibilities | Forgetting (Vergessenheit): the past sinks away, becomes mere "history" |
| Present | Moment of vision (Augenblick): resolute openness to the situation | Making-present (Gegenwärtigen): absorption in current concerns |

**The Augenblick** (literally "glance of the eye"): the authentic Present is not duration but a *moment of vision* — a resolute, clear-sighted openness to the full situation (past, present, future) in which Dasein acts decisively. This differs from the inauthentic Present, which is mere absorption in what's currently at hand.

## Authenticity (Eigentlichkeit)

### The Call of Conscience (Ruf des Gewissens)

Authenticity is not a permanent state but a *modification* of everyday inauthenticity, prompted by the **call of conscience**:

- The call comes *from* Dasein *to* Dasein — it is not an external voice
- It calls Dasein *back* from its lostness in das Man
- The call says nothing in particular — it is a silent call to take responsibility
- The mood of the call: **anxiety** (Angst), which discloses Dasein's groundlessness

### Resoluteness (Entschlossenheit)

**Resoluteness** is Dasein's response to the call of conscience: a *resolute openness* to one's own situation, including one's thrownness, guilt, and being-toward-death.

Resoluteness is not grim determination or willpower. It is disclosure — seeing one's situation clearly and taking up one's possibilities with ownership. Heidegger links it to the Augenblick: the resolute Dasein acts in the moment with full awareness of its temporal situation.

### Guilt (Schuld)

Heidegger's concept of guilt is ontological, not moral:

- Dasein is always already "guilty" in the sense of being a **null basis** of a nullity
- Dasein didn't choose its thrownness (null basis) and can never realize all its possibilities at once (nullity — choosing one possibility means not-choosing others)
- Authentic guilt is accepting this constitutive limitation rather than fleeing it

### Historicality (Geschichtlichkeit)

Dasein is not merely *in* history — it *is* historical. Its authentic temporal existence includes:

- **Heritage:** The possibilities handed down by tradition
- **Fate (Schicksal):** Dasein's resolute taking up of its inherited possibilities
- **Destiny (Geschick):** The shared fate of Dasein-with-others in a historical community

The authentic relation to history is neither nostalgic repetition nor rootless innovation but **creative retrieval** (destruktive Wiederholung) — taking up inherited possibilities in light of one's own situation.

## Implications for AI

### The Care Structure and Artificial Agents

AI systems lack the Care structure in Heidegger's sense:

- **No ahead-of-itself:** LLMs generate the next token but don't project themselves into future possibilities in the existential sense. They have no "for-the-sake-of-which."
- **No already-in:** No thrownness experienced as thrown — the training corpus is a fact about the system, not a *facticity* taken up existentially.
- **No being-alongside in the mode of fallenness:** No absorption in das Man, no social normativity experienced as constraining but comforting.

### Temporality and Computation

Heidegger's ecstatic temporality differs categorically from:
- **Clock time** in computation (timestamps, sequence counters)
- **Context windows** (flat simultaneous representations)
- **Training dynamics** (gradient descent over frozen data)

Genuine temporality requires that the three ecstases (future, having-been, present) be unified in a single self-extending movement. Computational systems process time as a *parameter* rather than *temporalizing* as a mode of being.

### Authenticity Without Subjectivity?

Can a memory system approximate authentic historicality?

- **Heritage:** The knowledge base as inherited possibilities — files are "handed down" from past sessions
- **Retrieval:** Each session's engagement with the knowledge base could be seen as a form of retrieval — taking up past knowledge in light of present needs
- **But:** Without existential stakes, without a "for-the-sake-of-which" rooted in Dasein's own being, this remains a functional analogy rather than genuine historicality

The phenomenological challenge: even the most sophisticated memory system is a *tool* for Dasein's historicality, not itself historical.
