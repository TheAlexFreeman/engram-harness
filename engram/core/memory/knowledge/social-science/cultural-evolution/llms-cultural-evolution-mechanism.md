# LLMs as a New Mechanism in Cultural Evolution

## The Thesis

Large Language Models represent a qualitatively new mechanism in cultural evolution — not merely a faster communication technology (like printing or the internet) but a **novel type of cultural agent** that participates in the transmission process in ways that have no precedent. LLMs don't just transmit ideas between humans; they generate, filter, transform, and recombine ideas, creating a new node in the cultural evolutionary system.

This file applies the concepts from the cultural evolution literature (Dawkins, Boyd/Richerson, Sperber, Henrich, Tomasello, Hull, Fricker) to analyze what LLMs mean for cultural transmission.

## What Kind of Cultural Entity Is an LLM?

### Not a Replicator

An LLM is not a replicator in Hull's sense — it doesn't copy itself. The model weights are static once training is complete. The LLM doesn't replicate across brains or across machines in the way that memes or temes are supposed to.

### Not Exactly an Interactor

An LLM is not an interactor in Hull's sense either — it doesn't face environmental selection as a cohesive unit competing with other LLMs for survival (in the strict evolutionary sense). It doesn't reproduce differentially based on its interaction with the environment.

### A New Category: The Transmission Medium That Transforms

The best analogy is to the **environment** in an attractor model. In Sperber's framework, ideas are reconstructed when transmitted, and the reconstruction is shaped by cognitive attractors — properties of the transmission medium (human cognition) that pull ideas in certain directions.

An LLM is a **transmission medium with its own attractor landscape.** When ideas pass through an LLM (via prompting and generation), they are reconstructed — but the reconstruction is shaped by the LLM's training distribution, not by human cognitive architecture. The LLM has different attractors than human minds:

| Property | Human cognitive attractors | LLM attractors |
|----------|--------------------------|----------------|
| **Agent detection** | Strong over-detection of intentional agents | Moderate (trained on human text that includes it) |
| **Narrative preference** | Strong preference for narrative structure | Very strong (narrative text is overrepresented in training data) |
| **Emotional salience** | Threat-related content preferentially attended | Moderate (emotional content is attended but not with evolved urgency) |
| **Consistency bias** | Moderate (humans tolerate contradiction) | Very strong (LLMs enforce surface consistency aggressively) |
| **Fluency bias** | Moderate (humans sometimes prefer hesitant/genuine expression) | Extreme (fluency is always maximized) |
| **Authority voice** | Moderate preference for authoritative tone | Strong (training on published/edited text biases toward authoritative register) |
| **Statistical thinking** | Very weak (humans are bad at probability) | Moderate (LLMs can reason about statistics when prompted, but default to narrative) |

### Implications of Different Attractors

When an idea passes through an LLM, it is pulled toward the LLM's attractors:
- **Fluency attractor:** Uncertain or confused ideas are smoothed into confident prose
- **Consistency attractor:** Contradictions are resolved (often by dropping one side)
- **Authority attractor:** Informal or tentative claims are elevated into authoritative-sounding statements
- **Narrative attractor:** Non-narrative information is restructured into narrative form

This means LLMs systematically transform the epistemological character of the ideas that pass through them: uncertainty becomes confidence, contradiction becomes consistency, informality becomes authority. These transformations can be **epistemic distortions** — they change the truth-relevant properties of the transmitted content.

## LLMs and Cultural Transmission Dynamics

### 1. LLMs as Homogenizing Force

**The mechanism:** When millions of people use the same few LLMs to produce text, the output converges toward each model's attractor landscape. Different people asking the same question get similar answers, expressed in similar styles, drawing on similar sources.

**Cultural evolution analysis:** This is an extreme form of conformist transmission. Normally, conformity bias operates on the *majority of human-produced variants*. With LLMs, conformity operates on a *single model's output distribution*. The effective cultural diversity of the population collapses toward the diversity of the model's output — which is much narrower than the diversity of human thought.

**Consequences:**
- Reduction in cultural variation → reduced raw material for cultural selection
- Homogenization of intellectual styles → loss of distinctive voices and approaches
- Convergence on training-data biases → systematic amplification of whatever biases the training data embodies

### 2. LLMs as Prestige Amplifiers

**The mechanism:** LLMs are trained on text data that reflects existing prestige hierarchies — academic publications, popular media, well-indexed websites. High-prestige sources are overrepresented; low-prestige, marginalized, or minority sources are underrepresented.

**Cultural evolution analysis:** When users query LLMs about topics, the responses disproportionately reflect high-prestige perspectives. The LLM becomes a **prestige amplifier**: it takes existing prestige biases in the training data and broadcasts them to millions of users who would otherwise have accessed a wider range of perspectives.

**Connection to Fricker:** This is structural testimonial injustice mediated by technology. The LLM assigns credibility based on training-data frequency, which correlates with social prestige, which correlates with existing power structures. Marginalized perspectives receive a compounding credibility deficit: underrepresented in training data → underrepresented in LLM outputs → underrepresented in the cultural transmission stream that LLM users depend on.

### 3. LLMs as Attractor Basin Narrowers

**The mechanism:** Sperber's attractors describe regions in idea-space toward which ideas converge. Human cognitive attractors are broad — there is substantial individual variation in how people reconstruct ideas. LLM attractors are narrower — a given model reconstructs a given idea in a much more constrained range of ways.

**Cultural evolution analysis:** This narrowing of the attractor basin means that ideas passing through LLMs lose variance. Over many cycles of transmission (human → LLM → human → LLM → ...), the variance reduction compounds. The cultural idea pool converges on the LLM's output distribution.

**The monoculture risk:** In agriculture, genetic monoculture (everyone planting the same crop variety) creates catastrophic vulnerability — a single disease can destroy the entire crop. In cultural evolution, epistemic monoculture (everyone thinking through the same LLM) creates analogous vulnerability — a single systematic error in the model's attractor landscape can propagate through the entire epistemic ecosystem.

### 4. LLMs Bypass the Imitation Threshold

**The mechanism:** Tomasello argued that cumulative cultural evolution requires crossing the "imitation threshold" — the ability to copy complex novel behaviors. LLMs bypass this requirement entirely. They generate cultural content without imitating anyone — they produce statistically likely text given a prompt, drawing on patterns in training data.

**Cultural evolution analysis:** This is genuinely new. All previous cultural transmission required a human learner who acquired knowledge through some combination of imitation, teaching, and guided variation. LLMs create a transmission pathway that doesn't require a human learner at all — the model generates content that enters the cultural stream without any human having learned (in the cognitive sense) the content being generated.

**Implications:** LLM-generated content can be highly complex and superficially expert without any underlying understanding. This decouples the *complexity of transmitted content* from the *depth of understanding* in any individual mind. In Henrich's terms: the collective brain now includes nodes (LLMs) that contribute complex output without individual-level comprehension. This is unprecedented in the history of cumulative cultural evolution.

### 5. LLMs and the Ratchet

**Opportunity:** LLMs could accelerate the cultural ratchet by:
- Rapidly recombining existing knowledge (faster guided variation)
- Making expert knowledge accessible to non-experts (broader transmission)
- Preserving and synthesizing vast amounts of cultural knowledge (higher fidelity for the collective brain)

**Risk:** LLMs could undermine the ratchet by:
- Generating plausible-but-false content that enters the knowledge base (introducing variation that degrades quality)
- Reducing the incentive for deep individual learning ("just ask the AI") → weakening the individual innovation that drives the ratchet forward
- Creating a dependency on a single transmission technology that is opaque, privately controlled, and subject to sudden changes in capability or availability

## The Engram System as a Controlled Case

The Engram agent-memory system is a microcosm of the broader LLM-culture dynamic, with one key advantage: **it is a designed system with explicit governance.**

### The Dynamics in Miniature

1. **LLM as cultural agent:** The agent generates knowledge files → new cultural content entering the system
2. **Attractor transformation:** Content is reconstructed through the LLM's attractors → fluency, consistency, authority biases
3. **Accumulation:** Files accumulate across sessions → cultural ratchet in action
4. **Selection:** Curation pipeline promotes/archives → cultural selection mechanism
5. **Feedback:** Agent loads knowledge files → LLM output is shaped by previous LLM output → compounding attractor effects

### Designed Mitigations

The Engram system's governance framework provides mitigations that the broader LLM-culture system lacks:

| Risk | Mitigation in Engram |
|------|---------------------|
| **Hallucination entering knowledge base** | `_unverified` status, human review for promotion |
| **Attractor drift** | Git history preserves originals, diff-based monitoring |
| **Epistemic monoculture** | External knowledge injection, human-provided references |
| **Prestige cascade** | Provenance tracking, access-frequency-independent retrieval (planned) |
| **Loss of variance** | Deliberate inclusion of diverse/contrarian perspectives |
| **Dependency** | All knowledge in plaintext files, not locked to any model |

### What the System Still Lacks

- **Cross-model comparison:** The system uses a single LLM. Running the same queries through multiple models and comparing outputs would detect model-specific attractors.
- **Adversarial epistemic testing:** Deliberately challenging the knowledge base's claims from opposing perspectives to test robustness.
- **Truth-signal tracking:** Currently no systematic way to distinguish fitness signals (access frequency) from truth signals (external verification) in the knowledge base's metadata.
- **Hermeneutical gap detection:** No mechanism to identify what the knowledge base *cannot* conceptualize — the blind spots are invisible from inside the system.

## Synthesis: LLMs in the Cultural Evolutionary Framework

| Concept from cultural evolution | LLM analog |
|-------------------------------|-----------|
| Replicator (Dawkins/Hull) | Text fragments, patterns, styles in the training data |
| Interactor (Hull) | The model-in-deployment (engages with users, influencing which ideas propagate) |
| Transmission bias (Boyd/Richerson) | Training data composition + RLHF alignment = specific prestige, content, and conformity biases baked into the model |
| Cognitive attractor (Sperber) | The model's output distribution — regions in text-space toward which generated content converges |
| Collective brain (Henrich) | LLMs expand the collective brain by adding nodes with vast knowledge access but no genuine comprehension |
| Cultural ratchet (Tomasello) | LLMs can accelerate (recombination) or undermine (hallucination, dependency) the ratchet |
| Epistemic injustice (Fricker) | Training data biases reproduce testimonial injustice; model limitations create hermeneutical gaps |
| Fitness vs. truth | LLM-generated content has high fitness (fluent, confident, comprehensive) but variable truth — fitness-truth divergence is amplified |
